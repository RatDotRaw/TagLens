Logseq knowledge base analysis

This project is generalized. You'll have to supply your own Logseq.

The goal of this visualization is to help you understand your own knowledge base and find new ways of separating and organize your knowledge base. By trying to give a birds eye view combined with your fine knowledge of whats inside Logseq, you might find new ways of achieving this. This can be done by looking for broader hashtags you could use for separating your knowledge base or drawing a line in your pages and splitting it into smaller graphs. (or both)

Later, you'll have the chance to make notes. Its important to write your reasoning down as wel. This later comes in handy when you're validating them to see whats important to you and whats not. You get a lot of aera to type in, maybe use it for once.

Know yourself and go scout in your logseq files. A lot of insights can be found by getting your hands dirty and by exploring and comparing the visual data with your logseq entries. It is important to take your time and jump back and forward in your logseq files and notes you'll be making.

as a quick side note; I'll be refering to 2 different pages types, journal and notes. when I talk about journal, I'm talking about pages from your journal in logseq (with a date in the title). when I talk about notes, I'm talking about pages without a date (everything except journal pages).
