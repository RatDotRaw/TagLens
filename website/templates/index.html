Logseq knowledge base analysis

This project is generalized. You'll have to supply your own Logseq.

The goal of this visualization is to help you understand your own knowledge base and find new ways of separating and organizing your knowledge base. By trying to give a bird's-eye view combined with your fine knowledge of what's inside Logseq, you might find new ways of achieving this. This can be done by searching for broader or overarching hashtags that encapsulate a bigger topic or theme , or finding clear points where you can separating your knowledge base into multiple smaller graphs. (or both)

Later, you'll have the chance to make notes. It's important to write your reasoning down as well. This later comes in handy when you're validating them to see what's important to you and what's not. You get a lot of area to type in, maybe use it for once.

Know yourself and go scout in your Logseq files. A lot of insights can be found by getting your hands dirty and by exploring and comparing the visual data with your Logseq entries. It is important to take your time and jump back and forth in your Logseq files and notes you'll be making.

As a quick side note; I'll be referring to 2 different page types, journal and notes. When I talk about journal, I'm talking about pages from your journal in Logseq (with a date in the title). When I talk about notes, I'm talking about pages without a date (everything except journal pages).
